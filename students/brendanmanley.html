Space Monkey

Under the cover of darkness I trudge through the marshes. Insects swarm, they slow my progress. Before the white mailbox, beach grass wavers in the cool summer night breeze. It beckons me. I see lights. Allowing myself to meld into the shadows, the lights pass, and I proceed. Pushing through the brush, a dilapidated wooden path from ages past is revealed. One foot in front of the other I gradually advance; the moon lights my trail. A steady incline ensues, followed by the inevitable peak. Gently stepping down I continue, however, a bit too hastily. Another peak is not far ahead. I fail to remember and stumble. Nearly losing my balance, I quickly regain my footing. Now, carefully moving onwards, I examine my surroundings: to my right an impressive, black fence shields the nearby property, discouraging entry, to my left there is all manner of growth, disallowing entry.
